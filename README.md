# What Claude Can’t See: The Jawbreaker Paper

**Product, Safety & Accessibility Proposals from a Non-Traditional Power User**

-----

## Why “The Jawbreaker Paper”?

Ideas are like jawbreakers. They start as a solid white ball. You don’t know what’s inside. You just know something is there.

You throw it at the wall. It cracks a little. One color shows. You throw it again. Another layer. You keep going, and each time more of the structure becomes visible. The colors were always there. You just couldn’t see them until the surface broke away.

That’s how every idea in this document was developed. I threw raw, unstructured thoughts at Claude. Claude acted as the wall. Each pass cracked the idea open a little more until the full architecture was visible. Then Claude translated what I found into professional language, and I could see my own thinking expressed in terms the experts use.

The wall doesn’t shape the idea. The wall reveals it.

## The Seven Concepts

1. **Claude Whiteboard** — A collaborative thinking mode where Claude acts as a wall to throw ideas against. You talk, Claude mirrors and translates, ideas get cracked open layer by layer. Not AI-generated ideas. A partner that helps you evolve your own.
1. **Simple Mode** — A domain translation layer that takes non-expert language, converts it to expert vocabulary, and shows both versions side by side. Bridges the gap between raw thinking ability and domain credibility.
1. **Claude Verify** — A separate Claude instance that validates outcomes against intent. Catches silently wrong code that passes all error checks by comparing actual results to what the user asked for.
1. **Claude Audit** — An asymmetric security tool that patches vulnerabilities without explaining exploits. Feeds patterns back to Anthropic for upstream model fixes and responsible disclosure.
1. **Trust Architecture** — Replace keyword filtering with reputation-based access, a constitution-only safety instance immune to user customization, proportional consequences, and a cross-platform bad actor registry.
1. **Compliance Workspace** — An ITAR/export-control-compliant environment with trust-gated access, guided compliance education, local-only encrypted storage, and dedicated infrastructure.
1. **Server-Side Claude Code with Secure Portability** — Run Claude Code sessions server-side so work persists when you close your laptop. Pick it up on your phone, interact with it anywhere. For compliance workspaces, the server itself becomes the secure “local” container. Deliverables can only be exported to verified compliant environments. If someone bypasses trust authentication and attempts to extract restricted materials through non-compliant channels, the system logs explicit intent, creating both a safety net and a legal record.

## How This Was Built

Twenty-two idea forks in three hours. Most of them produced something that went into the final document. Not a single thread was lost.

This entire paper was developed on a drive home from second shift, continued in a rental car with a dead laptop, powered by speech-to-text and whatever was left in the tank after eight hours of making plasma cutting parts.

Some things said during the session that I think belong here:

> “I always thought my rapid pace thought process and my lack of classic foundational formal education was a weakness. Turns out it’s my greatest strength.”

> “I’m not an engineer. That’s the actual qualification.”

> “Your ideas and thoughts are like a jawbreaker. You throw it at the wall, it cracks a little. You throw it again, another layer. The process goes on until you hit that beautiful center while still seeing the rainbow you drove through.”

> “It’s not there to do anything other than help you write your idea as it is meant to be. Taking your ideas and thoughts and showing you the value that you had inside all along.”

> “The toolset is the same. The usage changes based on the person.”

> “The only one who should suffer is the one who is trying to cause suffering.”

> “I can wake up and look at the same problem 100 times. And I’ll see, think, and feel about it in 100 different ways. And using Claude as my whiteboard, I can turn those into 300 new possible solutions.”
